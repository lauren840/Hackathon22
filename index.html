Beat the Heat


Who is at risk in extreme heat?

Children, athletes, older adults, pregnant people, outdoor workers and pets face the most risk from extreme heat. However, everyone can face adverse symptoms when temperatures get above 90 F (100% humidity) and 100 F (60% humidity).


What can I do to limit my risk?

Drink lots of water, find places with air conditioning, limit outdoor activity, and taking cool showers or baths can help. Find more information here.


How can I keep my pets cool?

You can do a few things to keep your pet safe such as: limiting their exercise on hot days, providing shade and iced water, and getting a cooling wrap/vest for them.


What are the signs of heat exhaustion?

Some symptoms of heat exhaustion are: cool/moist skin, heavy sweating, fainting, dizziness, fatigue, nausea, weak & rapid pulse, headache, muscle cramps, and low blood pressure on standing. You can treat it by stopping all activity, finding a cooler place, and drinking cold water or sports drinks.


What are the signs of heatstroke?

Some symptoms of heatstroke are: high body temperature, altered mentalstate/behavior, alteration in sweating, nausea and vomiting, flushed skin, rapid breathing, a racing heart rate, and a headache. Seek immediate medical help, while wating you should: get indoors, remove excess clothing, and coolyourself by whatever means possible such as a cool shower/bath, using ice packs, or wet towels.
